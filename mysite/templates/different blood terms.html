Different Blood Terms

Hemorrhage (bleeding): Blood leaking out of blood vessels may be obvious, as from a wound penetrating the skin...
Hematoma: A collection of blood inside the body tissues...
Leukemia: A form of blood cancer, in which white blood cells multiply abnormally...
Multiple myeloma: A form of blood cancer of plasma cells similar to leukemia...
Lymphoma: A form of blood cancer, in which white blood cells multiply abnormally inside lymph nodes...
Anemia: An abnormally low number of red blood cells in the blood...
Hemolytic anemia: Anemia caused by rapid bursting of large numbers of red blood cells...
Hemochromatosis: A disorder causing excessive levels of iron in the blood...
Sickle cell disease: A genetic condition in which red blood cells periodically lose their proper shape...
Bacteremia: Bacterial infection of the blood...
Malaria: Infection of red blood cells by Plasmodium...
Thrombocytopenia: Abnormally low numbers of platelets in the blood...
Leukopenia: Abnormally low numbers of white blood cells in the blood...
Disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC): An uncontrolled process of simultaneous bleeding and clotting...
Hemophilia: An inherited (genetic) deficiency of certain blood clotting proteins...
Hypercoaguable state: Numerous conditions can result in the blood being prone to clotting...
Polycythemia: Abnormally high numbers of red blood cells in the blood...
Deep venous thrombosis (DVT): A blood clot in a deep vein, usually in the leg...
Myocardial infarction (MI): Commonly called a heart attack...
